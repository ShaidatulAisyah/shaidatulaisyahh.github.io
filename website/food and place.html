Other than that, i love spaghetti. It really made my day when i have it. In general, the cooking time listed on the package of your pasta is a good, if approximate, place to start. I usually test my pasta about a minute or two early, just to be safe. Simply fish a piece of pasta out of the pot and place it on your cutting board to cool slightly. Be careful as it will be very hot! I often cut it in half to make it easier to taste. Cutting the pasta will also gives me a sense of how firm it still is and will tell me if it is even close to being ready. If the pasta seems soft enough, I pop it into my mouth as a final test. It should be chewy without any crunch and no longer taste raw — cooked pasta has an almost sweet flavor. While many people like their pasta al dente, if you like it a little softer and less chewy, then by all means cook your pasta that way! Additionally, if you are serving it with a saucy sauce such as a bolognese or red sauce, you may want to finish cooking the pasta by simmering it in the sauce for a minute or two. If that’s the case, be sure to remove the pasta from the water when it still has a little bit of crunch in the very middle so that it can finish cooking in the sauce without over cooking.
